Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Interlligence

Emotional Intelligence or EQ (emotional quotient) is an understanding and recognition of emotions. It is the ability to understand your emotions and emotional state, as well as the emotional states of those around you. Some models describe is as having four abilities:

  • Perceiving Emotions - The ability to recogise emotions in yourself and others.
  • Using Emotions - The ability to use or change your emotional state.
  • Understanding Emotions - The ability to understand differences in emotions and the connections between emotions.
  • Managing Emotions - The ability to regulate emotions in yourself and others.

The Difference Between EQ and IQ

IQ can be roughly summed up as your ability to reason and problem solve. Where as EQ can be summed up as your ability to precieve the emotional states of yourself and those around you. IQ might help you solve a problem but EQ will help you deal with emotions while you are solving the problem.

Why is EQ important?

Because you will often be working with people, being able to understand and empathise with others would make you an incredibly helpful teammate. And while IQ might help problem solve abstract problems, EQ will help you solve interpersonal problems. It also allows for emotional regulation and the ability manage strong emotions like stress, anger, and frustration. The ability to look inside and discern how you are feeling, what made you feel this way, and how you can change how your feeling, is an invaluable skill for anyone to have.

How to do engage and practice mindfulness?

Mindfulness is a skill of metacognition. Metacognition is a understanding of your own thought processes, any patterns in them and what may cause them. Mindfulness can be cultivated and practiced. Some ways to practice are:

  • Guided Meditation
  • Body scans
  • Journaling
