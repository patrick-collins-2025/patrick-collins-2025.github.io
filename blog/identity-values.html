Identity and Values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

A situation where I made an ethical decision. I'm not sure. I have really struggled to think of a specific decision but I would hope most decisions I make are ethical. The choice to boycott certain companies because of their on going support for genocide. Or going to protests against an anti-trans activist. These decisions I think are weighed up pretty easily, not buying a certain product and the supermarket or giving up a few hours out of my weekend to show support for a marginalised group of people. When I reflect on the decisions I feel that they were the correct ones and a rather easy trade off to make.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I think the people around me have influenced my values and identity hugely, the things I believe about myself and the world around me are directly impacted by how my parents raised me and who I have chosen to be surrounded by. My world view and my views on others, the belief of equality for all and the role capitalism does or does not have to play in it, have all been shaped by parents both directly and indirectly. Being white I feel a distinct lack of culture, no doubt it is there and that I have been shaped by it. But like what is White New Zealander culture? I dunno, pies maybe? I feel like this is a considerable more complicated topic than I have the knowledge or expertise for. But definitely my friends and family and teachers and pies have influenced my identity and values immeasurable.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I think I am deeply curious, I love to find out more about things, I love to know things, I love factoids. I really enjoy falling down a good wikipedia rabbit hole. I think this will help me in my learning journey to discover new things and ways of creating new things. As long as I can point my desire to learn things towards web development I should be okay.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I am very prone to procrastination. I really tend to not do things until the last moment when I begin to feel the pressure of an approaching deadline. Which is something I would like to change. I read (a headline of a reddit post) that procrastinating is linked to anxiety, which makes sense for me at least. And in my strengths I talked about my desire to learn new things may limit my learning journey if I'm not able to constructively aim and and make sure I am not getting off topic or too into the weeds. I do think this is where timeboxing will help me a lot.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

When I worked in a kitchen as a dishwasher and as a chef, you have to work productively with people everyday. However there can be a lot of tension. Tension between people in the kitchen, between chefs needing items done to serve food, tension between chefs and dishwashers needing certain plates or utensils cleaned or ready for service. As well as tension between the kitchen at large and the other parts of the business, management, front of house, customers. Strategies I used at the time, especally in more junior roles is to just go with the flow. Often I would be told contridictory information from different chefs and I found it was by far the easiest to just do it how they wanted. It was incredibly effective in terms of avoiding conflict and getting immediate work done. However it definitely wasn't the most effective for getting work done in the long run as it created a lot of tension points between chefs and would impact the teams ability to get work done. Now I think I would take a much more straight forward approach to these things, even if they might create conflict in the short term because I think they would create a much better environment for productivity.
