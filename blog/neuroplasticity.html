Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

What is Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is how your brain is able to grow and reorganise itself. The ability for the brain to rewire connections, create new connections, or strengthen existing connections. Once thought to only exist in childhood, neuroplasticity exists in everyone and is an important part of learning.

What is a Growth Mindset

A Growth Mindset is the idea that your ability and skill originates from learning, and persevering through challenges, and that failure is an important part of learning new information and skills. This is opposed by the fixed mindset that believes that ability and skill is more or less static and little can be done to change that. And that failure is the ultimate barrier between them and success.

What are the benefits

Neuroplasticity:

The ability for the brain to rewire, and strength its neural connections is important for learning new skills and absorbing new information. But that would happen with or without the knowledge of neuroplasticity, the importance of the knowledge of neuroplasticity is that you now know by practice and repetition you are able to learn things that may have seemed beyond your ability before, which hopefully will give you more grit and staying power when learning new and difficult things.

Growth Mindset:

The benefits of a growth mindset are that you can overcome difficult and new challenges and fail at them while still knowing that it is part of your learning journey. Under a fixed mindset, believing that failure is the benchmark of your static ability, will stop you learning, even if you are learning from your failures.

How can you increase your Neuroplasticity and use your Growth Mindset

Practice, repetition, connection of new knowledge to already existing knowledge and skill, all these things will increase and strengthen the neural connections in your brain, making further learning of skills and information in that area easier and increasing ability. And to use your growth mind set you need to trust that if you stick with it, even if you fail, in the long run will have greater ability than if you gave up and didn't fail.
