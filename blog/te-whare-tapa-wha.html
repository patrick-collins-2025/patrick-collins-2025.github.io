Te Whare Tapa Wha

Te whare tapa wha - the house with four walls - is a holistic approach to health and wellbeing, developed by Ta Mason Durie in 1984. - Here are some of my plans to stay healthy in these five aspects:

  • taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
  • taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  • taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  • taha whanau (family wellbeing)
  • Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

  • a minimum half hour walk everyday.
  • and some strenuous exercise at least once a week.

Taha Hinengaro (mental wellbeing)

  • meditation before to start the day in the right head space.
  • journaling at the end of the day to decompress and consider what I have done during the day.

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

  • Graditude journal every day in the morning. Although studies show this might positively effect multiple walls of the house

Taha Whanau (family wellbeing)

  • Spending time with my niece once a week to play boardgames.
  • A family dinner at least once every two weeks.

>Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

  • A walk in nature at least once a week.
  • Bird watching on said walks.
